Plasma Tattoo Removal

What to Know Before You Start

Tattoo removal is a gradual process regardless of the technology used. Plasma removal works by using ionised gas to break down the ink pigment in the skin over multiple sessions, allowing the body's immune system to clear the fragmented particles progressively.

Setting realistic expectations from the beginning is important. Results depend on several factors that vary between clients:

  • Ink type, colour, and density
  • Depth of the original tattoo
  • Location on the body and skin thickness in that area
  • The body's individual healing and immune response
  • How well aftercare is followed between sessions

Session Scope

Each session treats an area of up to 4cm x 3cm. For small tattoos that fall within this size, the full design can be treated in a single session pass. For larger tattoos, the design is divided into sections — each section is worked on in rotation, with adequate healing time in between.

A minimum of seven sessions is typically required. The total number depends on the variables above and is assessed on an ongoing basis as the tattoo responds to treatment.

The Process

  1. The tattoo is assessed — size, ink characteristics, skin condition. A treatment plan and realistic session estimate is discussed.
  2. The treatment area is cleaned and prepared. A topical numbing agent is applied where appropriate.
  3. The plasma device is worked across the treatment area, breaking down ink pigment section by section.
  4. Post-treatment care is applied. Detailed aftercare instructions are provided — this step is critical to how the skin heals between sessions.
  5. Follow-up timing is confirmed. Adequate healing time between sessions is non-negotiable for safe, progressive removal.

Between Sessions

The period between sessions is where the actual removal happens. The body processes the fragmented ink particles through its lymphatic system over the weeks following each session. Rushing the interval between treatments does not accelerate the process — it undermines it. The schedule between sessions is based on how your skin heals, not a fixed calendar.
